\section*{Multiplying Integers}

Today we'll learn to multiply two numbers with various signs.

This table shows the shows the sign of the product of two numbers of signs given by the row and column headers.  For now it will suffice to memorize it.

\begin{table}[h!]
\centering
\begin{tabular}{ |c||c|c| }
 \hline
* & + & - \\
 \hline \hline
 + & + & - \\
 \hline
 - & - & + \\
 \hline
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

These rules are the same for division, because division is multiplication.

    \item \((3 * (-2)) + (3 * 2)=\) \vspace{1em}
    \item \((5 * 1) + ((-1) * 4) = \) \vspace{1em}
    \item \(10 * (-2) + 3 =\) \vspace{1em}
    \item \((4 / 2) * (-2) =\) \vspace{1em}
    \item \((-10) / 2 =\) \vspace{1em}

    \item For arbitrary numbers x and y, does \(x * y = y * x\) ? \vspace{1em}

\end{enumerate}
